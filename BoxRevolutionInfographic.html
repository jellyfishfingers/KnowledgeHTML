The Origin Story

1937: The "Aha!" Moment

Before the shipping container, global trade was a logistical nightmare known as "break-bulk" shipping. Goods were packed in sacks, barrels, and wooden crates.

Malcolm McLean, a trucker from North Carolina, spent days waiting at ports, watching dockworkers manually haul cotton bales one by one. He realized the inefficiency wasn't just annoying‚Äîit was expensive. His radical idea? Lift the whole truck trailer onto the ship.
